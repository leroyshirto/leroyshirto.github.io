It's been about nine years since I last posted anything here. Nine years! In internet time that's roughly three geological eras and at least five JavaScript frameworks ago.

But here I am, dusting off the cobwebs and firing up a fresh Hugo site. The old blog served me well — it saw me through university projects, Android apps, Unity hacking, and whatever WordPress-to-Hugo migration crimes I committed back in 2016. Now it's time for a new chapter.

## So what's changed?

Well, quite a lot. Professionally I've been deep in the world of **Kubernetes** and **Docker** — wrangling containers, writing Helm charts, debugging networking issues at 2am, and generally trying to keep things running in production. If you've ever stared at a CrashLoopBackOff and questioned your life choices, we'll get along just fine.

I'll be writing about the stuff I work with day to day:

- **Kubernetes** — cluster operations, platform engineering, the good bits and the bits that make you want to throw your laptop out of a window
- **Docker** — container best practices, multi-stage builds, and why your image is 2GB when it should be 50MB
- **AI and LLMs** — the space is moving at a ridiculous pace right now. From coding assistants to agents to whatever drops next week, I want to dig into what's actually useful versus what's just hype
- **DevOps and platform engineering** — CI/CD, GitOps, infrastructure as code, and all the glue that holds modern software delivery together

## Why now?

Honestly? The AI stuff tipped me over the edge. The tooling has got so good that it felt criminal not to be documenting the journey. Plus I've accumulated nine years of opinions and war stories that need to go somewhere other than Slack threads that disappear into the void.

No promises on a posting schedule — I learned that lesson last time — but I've got plenty to talk about.

Good to be back.
